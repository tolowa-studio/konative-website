# Konative two-lane market briefing — STORM verified (2026-07-06)

Multi-perspective research (7 lenses: practitioner, economist, skeptic, historian,
tribal buyer, data-center buyer, competitive/web) with adversarial citation
verification against primary sources. Every load-bearing number below survived
verification; corrections from the verify pass are already applied.

## 60-second summary

Both lanes are real, but neither pays the way the "funding is coming in September"
premise assumed — and the corrected picture is **better**, not worse.

**Tribal:** September 17, 2026 is not an award date — it is the **application
deadline** for $790M in brand-new NTIA money (TBCP Round 3, $540M + Native
Entities Grant Program, $250M, opened June 17, 2026; awards roll from Spring
2027). That means July–September 2026 is peak grant-writing season in Indian
Country, an acute and time-boxed moment when help is scarce and giving it builds
trust. Grant dollars themselves never pay a broker commission — the commissionable
revenue is (a) tribal *enterprise* connectivity (casinos, government, health,
education) that buys circuits **today** regardless of grants, and (b) grant-built
networks that become transit/circuit buyers in 2027+.

**Data center:** the boom's connectivity pain is genuine and broker-shaped —
fiber is the new bottleneck, laterals kill project IRR, and the boom has moved to
secondary markets (Columbus, Salt Lake City, San Antonio, Reno, Alberta) with the
thinnest carrier density. But hyperscalers buy direct. The agent-addressable
market is the **ecosystem around builds**: developers needing laterals and
carrier attraction, enterprises/AI companies deploying into colo, regional
operators needing diverse routes. A correction in the boom *increases* broker
value (repricing, churn, distressed-capacity arbitrage — the post-2002 precedent).

**The web/authority gap:** every agency competitor site is brochureware; none has
a single public tool; none executes AEO (llms.txt, FAQPage, Speakable). No
dominant national advisory brand owns "tribal connectivity brokerage" or
"data-center connectivity procurement" search categories (small brokers rank, but
no authority). One honest tool per lane + published benchmarks + full AEO
discipline is the moat — the E-Rate precedent (Funds For Learning) shows
published-data authority compounds for 25 years.

## Key findings, ranked by reliability

**F1 — The September window is an application wave, not an award wave. (10/10 — NTIA primary sources)**
NTIA opened two NOFOs June 17, 2026: TBCP Round 3 ($540M; infrastructure +
use/adoption incl. affordability subsidies) and the Digital Equity Native
Entities Grant Program ($250M; grants $500K–$2.5M; 10% match, waivable). Both due
**Sept 17, 2026, 11:59 p.m. ET**; rolling awards from Spring 2027.
Source: [NTIA press release](https://www.ntia.gov/press-release/2026/ntia-announces-two-new-funding-opportunities-expand-broadband-connectivity-tribal-lands), [TBCP3 NOFO](https://broadbandusa.ntia.gov/sites/default/files/2026-06/NTIA_TBCP3_NOFO.pdf).

**F2 — Grant money ≠ broker commission; two revenue clocks. (9/10 — supported by skeptic + practitioner + economist, unanimous)**
TBCP/BEAD fund construction capex by grantees/ISPs; there is no commission line
for a sub-agent in a grant. Commissionable spend: recurring circuits (middle-mile
transit, enterprise WAN, DIA, UCaaS, security). Clock A (now): tribal enterprise
IT — casinos, tribal government, health systems — buys today. Clock B (2027+):
TBCP/BEAD-built networks become transit and enterprise-circuit buyers 12–36
months behind the awards. Grant help is lead-gen and trust-building, never the
revenue line.

**F3 — TBCP context: oversubscribed, politically volatile, and NOT done. (9/10)**
275 awards / $2.2B obligated of ~$3B; ~$980M was unobligated before the new NOFO;
~$1B in Round 2 payments were delayed (Cantwell/Schatz Senate letter). Historian:
Round 1 drew $5B+ in requests against $980M initially available. A July-2026
entrant is **early for the next wave**, not late for the last one.
Source: [Senate Commerce](https://www.commerce.senate.gov/press/dem/release/cantwell-schatz-demand-answers-on-trump-administrations-delay-of-nearly-1-billion-in-tribal-broadband-grants/).

**F4 — BEAD weakened tribal leverage; tribes must engage state offices or get planned around. (9/10 — corrected)**
All 56 Final Proposals submitted; 54 approved; construction starts summer 2026.
Two separate waivers matter: the subpoint (E) waiver *eliminates* tribal consent
for near-reservation areas, and a separate programmatic waiver gives states 6
months post-approval to obtain Resolutions of Consent on actual tribal lands.
Advisory demand exists here, but it is consulting-shaped, not commission-shaped.

**F5 — DC connectivity pain is real, in specific places. (8/10)**
~35 GW under construction in North America; Goldman projects +13.6 GW (2026) and
+36.3 GW (2027); ~half of planned 2026 US builds face delay/cancellation on
power/transformer shortages — both things are true at once. AI DCs need up to 36x
more fiber; cable lead times run 20 weeks (large buyers) to a year (small); FBA
says US fiber miles must ~2.3x by 2029. A 5-mile lateral can kill deal economics;
metro dark fiber is concentrated in NoVA/SV/Chicago/Dallas — exactly not where
the boom moved. Hard capex number: Big-4 hyperscaler 2026 guidance ≈ $700–725B.

**F6 — Who actually pays a sub-agent, and how much. (8/10 — corrected)**
Connectivity residuals 15–20% of MRC (carrier range 5–25%), evergreen for account
life *if the contract says so* — the evergreen clause is the single most
load-bearing term in the AVANT sub-agent agreement. TSD pass-through to
sub-agents: best-sourced range **50–70%** (higher tiers exist but are not
citable). Effective take ≈ 8–14% of customer MRC. Deal sizes: 1G DIA $700–1,500
MRC; 10G DIA $4,000–7,500; 100G metro waves low thousands. Dark fiber IRUs pay
one-time, not residual. Average advisor multi-homes across ~2.9 TSDs (median 2).
Commission starts only after install: DIA 60–120 days post-signature; tribal
deals 12–24+ months end-to-end.

**F7 — The buyer demand for advisors is documented and growing. (8/10)**
AVANT's study: 84% of IT buyers use a trusted advisor; Telarus: ~93% of CIOs rely
on or want one. TSD market $16.6B (+14.5%), top six = 72.3% share; AVANT ~$2.1B
revenue, took Court Square + Pamlico growth investment **Dec 17, 2025**. AVANT
supplies Pathfinder (QuickQuote 20+ providers, provider analytics from 1,700+
advisor/client survey responses), an SE bench, and contracting — a sub-agent's
irreducible job is originating opportunities.

**F8 — Tribal trust architecture decides everything in Lane 1. (8/10)**
Tribes evaluate partners on ownership-vs-vendorship (tribe as owner, not
customer), data sovereignty as explicit procurement criteria, Native
identity/endorsement, and in-person presence (TribalNet, Sept 20–24, 2026,
Hilton Anatole, Dallas — 700+ tribal tech leaders). Fresh fraud headlines (DOJ
FCA suit, March 2026, >$2.1M on the Rosebud Sioux broadband project) raise the
bar for every outside firm. The trusted-advisor set (AMERIND, Tribal Ready,
MuralNet, NTUA, EnerTribe) are **partners, not competitors** — they do funding
and builds; none do multi-carrier procurement. A broker who feeds them deals gets
endorsed; an outsider competing head-on gets frozen out.

**F9 — Tribal×DC convergence is a real, contested, ownable intersection. (7/10)**
100+ proposed DC projects on/near Native lands (Honor the Earth tracking); DOE
Office of Indian Energy is actively encouraging tribal data-center partnerships;
NTUA operates a (self-characterized) Tier-3 DC in Shiprock on its 550-mile
(now 800+) fiber backbone; Arcadian Infracom holds BIA-approved easement for its
Phoenix–Salt Lake City route through Navajo Nation. Sovereign permitting can
compress timelines — but organized opposition exists; consent-first,
tribe-side-advocacy framing is mandatory. **Konative is nearly alone in being
credible in both lanes at once.**

**F10 — The website gap: no agency has a tool; nobody executes AEO. (7/10 — corrected)**
UPSTACK/Bridgepointe/Bluewave: brochureware + people directories. Lightyear owns
the category SERPs via its /tips content moat but is horizontal SaaS with zero
tribal/federal-funding fluency. AVANT/Telarus tools are advisor-gated — end
buyers landing on goavant.net find nothing for them; the sub-agent can own the
buyer-facing layer. Correction from verification: small brokers *do* rank for
"dark fiber broker" (Point Broker, Nationwide Bandwidth) and procurement
consultancies for "data center connectivity procurement" (Procure IT, 515
Engine) — the accurate claim is **no dominant national advisory brand owns these
categories**, and none of the rankers has Konative's data assets or dual-lane story.

## Contradiction map (where lenses disagreed, and the resolution)

1. **"Sept 2026 funding" vs. NOFO reality** → September is the application
   deadline; show up before the money. Better than the premise.
2. **Grant dollars vs. commissions** → two clocks (enterprise now, grant-built
   networks 2027+); grants = lead-gen.
3. **DC boom vs. bubble** → sell to the ecosystem around builds in secondary
   markets, not hyperscalers; brokerage is anti-fragile to a correction
   (post-2002 precedent).
4. **Website: credential vs. channel** → build a data/authority engine, not a
   brochure; the tool and published benchmarks make referrals convert and get
   cited (Funds For Learning precedent). Referrals still originate most deals —
   the site's job is to convert and compound them.
5. **Tribal gap vs. tribal wall** → enter as the procurement layer that feeds
   Native-owned advisors; sovereignty commitment in writing; faces, not forms.

## The assumption this briefing rests on

That Konative's AVANT sub-agent agreement secures **evergreen residuals and
assignability** of the book. The historian lens is unambiguous: the book is the
asset (books trade at ~1–1.5x annual residuals; UPSTACK exists to buy them).
Verify those two clauses before scaling origination.

## The missing lens

**Legal/regulatory:** tribal procurement law (TERO, Buy Indian Act) implications
for a non-Native broker serving tribes; carrier contract law on
evergreen/assignment. Recommend a focused follow-up before the first tribal
contract signature.
