# Konative two-lane GTM strategy (2026-07-06)

Companion to `2026-07-06-market-storm-briefing.md` (verified market facts) and
the website blueprint. This is the operating plan.

## Positioning (one sentence per audience)

- **To a tribal leader:** "Konative is the vendor-neutral procurement arm that
  makes carriers compete for your enterprise and your network — you own the
  contracts, the data, and the relationship; the suppliers pay us."
- **To a data-center developer/operator/tenant:** "Konative sources the network
  into your facility — laterals, waves, transit, cross-connects — across the
  whole carrier market, with the route and market data to prove the price is right."
- **Category claim (site + AEO):** *North America's connectivity brokerage for
  Tribal Nations and the data-center buildout.* Nobody else can say both halves.

## The two clocks (revenue architecture)

| Clock | What | When it pays | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — Tribal enterprise | Casinos, tribal gov, health, education WAN/DIA/UCaaS/security | Now (install + 60–120 days) | Revenue engine, Lane 1 |
| A — DC ecosystem | Enterprises into colo, developer laterals, regional-operator routes | Now (60–180 days) | Revenue engine, Lane 2 |
| B — Grant-built networks | TBCP/BEAD networks buying transit, middle-mile, enterprise circuits | 2027–2029 | Pipeline seeded by help given in 2026 |
| B — Tribal×DC projects | 100+ tribal-adjacent DC proposals; DOE-encouraged partnerships | 2027+ | The convergence moat — only Konative sits in both rooms |

Grant application help (TBCP R3 / NEGP, due **Sept 17, 2026**) is **free and
public** — it is the trust purchase for Clock B, never a revenue line.

## Lane 1 — Tribal (the trust lane)

**Q3 2026 calendar is the strategy:**
1. **Now → Sept 17:** Ship the free TBCP-R3/NEGP Funding Navigator on the site
   (deadline countdown, eligibility walkthrough, eligible-uses explainer,
   application checklist). Publish, then push to the 4,410 tribal CRM contacts.
2. **Sept 20–24:** TribalNet, Hilton Anatole, Dallas — 700+ tribal tech leaders,
   three days after they file. Konative attends with one artifact: the printed
   benchmark card ("what tribes pay for DIA/transport") + the navigator QR.
3. **Q4 2026:** follow-up sequence to every navigator user and TribalNet contact:
   free connectivity cost benchmark against their current carrier quotes
   (enterprise circuits — commissionable Clock A work).
4. **Spring 2027:** awards start rolling — every applicant Konative helped now
   needs procurement. Public RFP monitoring (Tulalip-pattern) begins.

**Trust architecture (non-negotiable, from the buyer lens):**
- **Sovereignty commitment page, in writing:** tribe owns the contracts, the
  data, and the relationship; Konative is agent-of-record working for the tribe,
  not the carrier; transparent broker economics (suppliers pay 15–20% of MRC;
  no lock-ins; walk away anytime).
- **Partnership architecture:** AMERIND, Tribal Ready, MuralNet, NTUA, tribal
  grant consultants are partners — Konative provides the multi-carrier
  quoting/lifecycle layer none of them has, and feeds them the funding/build
  work. Target: one Native-owned partner endorsement on the site by Q4.
- **Faces, not forms:** named humans, in-person presence, and the Tolowa story
  told plainly — authentic, personal, front-and-center.

**CRM activation (4,410 tribal contacts in Twenty):**
- Segment by role: tribal IT/CIO → navigator + benchmarks; tribal council/admin
  → funding deadline awareness; tribal enterprise (casino/health) ops → Clock A
  circuit audit offer.

  Pure help, zero pitch.

## Lane 2 — Data center (the speed lane)

**Target buyers (in order of realistic close speed):**
1. Enterprises/AI companies deploying into colo — waves, DIA, transit,
   cross-connects (fastest; supplier bench: Arelion, FiberLight, Cologix, Zayo,
   Lumen via AVANT).

   attraction, diverse routes, laterals (Columbus, SLC, San Antonio, Reno,
   Indianapolis, Alberta, Montreal).
3. Tribal×DC convergence — tribe-side advocacy on the 100+ tribal-adjacent
   proposals (NTUA/Arcadian as the model; consent-first framing always).

**Motion:**
- **Artifact-led:** publish the secondary-market fiber briefs + benchmark tables
  (lateral $/ft by region, published-range IRU + wave pricing with honest "custom
  quote" caveats). One honest benchmark table beats any brochure — and nobody
  else publishes one.
- **Referral engine:** site-selection consultants, owner's reps, MSPs, and DC
  construction firms get a no-conflict referral lane (they keep their scope;
  Konative takes the network only). Mirror of the C4/MSP pattern.
- **CRM activation (remaining ~4,000 contacts):** re-segment by data-center
  relevance (developers, operators, enterprises with colo footprints — Apollo
  enrichment Phase 4 feeds this). Sequence: market brief → lit-building/route
  question ("which carriers can reach your site? we'll pull it") → 15-min call.
- **Deal registration discipline:** register every opportunity in AVANT
  Pathfinder immediately; use the SE bench on every technical call (rentable
  credibility).

## Revenue model (honest base case, from verified economics)

- Effective take ≈ 8–14% of customer MRC (15–20% residual × 50–70% pass-through).
- First residuals month 6–7; UCaaS SPIFFs ($3–8k one-time) bridge cash.
- Exit-2026 book target: $18–25k MRC under management ≈ $26–36k ARR run-rate.
- One anchor deal (tribal casino/gov WAN $5–10k MRC, or a 100G wave pair)
  moves year-one cash to $40–60k and run-rate past $60k.
- Solo break-even ≈ $80–100k MRC ≈ 24–30 months. Tribal Clock B pays years 2–3.
- **Contract check before scaling:** evergreen + assignability clauses in the
  AVANT sub-agent agreement (the book is the asset; UPSTACK exists to buy books
  at ~1–1.5x annual residuals).

## Risks and mitigations (from the skeptic, kept visible)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| NTIA freezes/rewrites programs again | Revenue never depends on grants; Clock A is grant-independent |
| DC pipeline slippage (~half of 2026 builds) | Sell to ecosystem, not projects; brokerage is anti-fragile to repricing/churn |
| Outsider distrust in Indian Country | Sovereignty page, partner-first architecture, in-person, Tolowa story; never grant-farm |
| Lightyear-style disintermediation | Stay in complex/relationship deals; publish data they can't (tribal + secondary-market) |
| Roll-up commoditization | The data layer + dual-lane authority is the moat generalist books lack |
