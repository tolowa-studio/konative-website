## 0. TL;DR (the one-paragraph version)

Konative is becoming an **internet connectivity brokerage** — a vendor-neutral
agent that sources, designs, and manages network connectivity for the buyers the
carriers underserve. We operate as a **subagent under Avant Communications** (a

advantage is the **proprietary data-center and market dataset we already built** —
it tells us where connectivity demand is landing before anyone else knows. The
data-center *data* stops being the product; it becomes the **lead engine** for the
brokerage. Nothing on the current site gets deleted — the message gets re-ranked
so konative.com reads, instantly, as an elite connectivity brokerage.

### 2.1 The TSD / agent channel model

- The old "master agent" label has been rebranded to **Technology Services
  Distributor (TSD)**. The majors are **Avant, Telarus, Intelisys (ScanSource),
  Sandler Partners, and AppDirect**. TSDs aggregate carrier/supplier contracts,
  back-office, tooling, and commissions; **subagents (a.k.a. sales partners /
  trusted advisors)** like Konative own the customer relationship and the sale.
- **How a subagent earns:** monthly **residual commissions** on the customer's
  recurring spend (typically a percentage of MRC), often **evergreen** (paid for
  the life of the account) plus upfront **SPIFFs/bonuses** from suppliers. The
  agent carries no inventory and signs no direct carrier paper — the TSD holds the
  master agreements.
- **Why buyers use an agent instead of going direct:** vendor-neutral sourcing
  across many carriers at once, a single point of contact for quoting/ordering/
  escalations, lifecycle support (the agent stays after the sale), and **no cost to
  the buyer** — the supplier pays the commission, so advisory is effectively free.
- **What distinguishes Avant specifically:** Avant leans on **data and tooling** as
  its differentiator — **Pathfinder** (its quoting/sourcing platform), **BattleApps**,
  market analytics, and its annual **Special Forces Summit**. Avant's pitch to its
  own agents is "sell smarter with data." → **This is a perfect cultural fit for a
  data-driven brokerage like Konative.** We should mirror Avant's data-first
  language in our pitch to them.

### 2.2 The product portfolio a connectivity agent sells

Dedicated internet access (DIA), broadband, **lit & dark fiber**, **wavelengths**,
MPLS/VPLS, **SD-WAN**, **UCaaS/CCaaS**, mobility / wireless WAN / **FWA**, POTS
replacement, **colocation**, **cloud on-ramps / direct connect**, **network &
data-center interconnection (DCI)**, cybersecurity, and managed services.

**Best margin/growth for a broker:** recurring, sticky, high-MRC categories —
**dark fiber & wavelengths** (high value, multi-year), **SD-WAN** and **UCaaS/CCaaS**
(fast-growing, strong residuals), and **cloud connectivity / DCI** (riding the AI
buildout). Commodity broadband is low-margin and best used as a door-opener.

- ⚠️ **Dark fiber market:** ~**$8.9B (2025) → ~$29.9B (2035)**, ~**12.9% CAGR**;
  North America ≈ 32% share; U.S. ≈ $2.0B (2025) → ~$7.0B (2035).
  *(Precedence Research.)*
- ⚠️ **SD-WAN market:** ~**$5.9B (2024) → ~$42.6B (2030)**, ~**38.9% CAGR**.
  *(P&S Market Research.)*

### 2.3 The Tribal connectivity wedge

This is the differentiator no other Avant subagent owns at scale.

- **NTIA Tribal Broadband Connectivity Program (TBCP):** a **~$3B** federal program
  — **$1B** from the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2021 plus **$2B** from the
  Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act — funding Tribal governments for high-speed
  internet, telehealth, distance learning, affordability, and adoption.
  *(NTIA.)*
- **Eligibility is Tribal-only:** Tribal Governments, Tribal Colleges & Universities,
  the Dept. of Hawaiian Home Lands (Native Hawaiian Community), Tribal Organizations,
  and Alaska Native Corporations — i.e., a **sovereignty-aligned** program.
  *(NTIA.)* This is exactly the trust posture a Tribal-focused broker must embody.
- ⚠️ **Program status (late 2025):** ~**275 TBCP awards** totaling ~**$2.2B**
  (226 projects / ~$1.87B in the first NOFO impacting 400+ Tribes; 48 projects /
  ~$360M in the second). A **new combined NOFO is planned for Spring 2026** with at
  least ~**$500M** remaining (merging TBCP with the Digital Equity Act native set-
  aside). *(NTIA; U.S. Senate Commerce Committee.)*
- **BEAD** (the ~$42.5B state broadband program) carries Tribal coordination/
  consent requirements, adding more Tribal-adjacent network spend.
- ⚠️ **The gap that creates demand:** as of end-2018, only ~**60%** of residents on
  tribal lands (lower 48) had broadband access — a persistent rural/Tribal divide.
  *(Native News Online.)*
- **Why Tribal enterprises are underserved & why a sovereignty-aware broker wins:**
  carriers historically skip low-density Tribal lands; Tribal **gaming/casinos,
  government, healthcare, and education** enterprises need carrier-grade connectivity,
  redundancy, and security but lack a trusted, neutral advisor who understands
  sovereignty, jurisdiction, and federal funding. **Konative's founder background
  and the "KO-NATIVE" brand position us authentically here.** (Tolowa Pacific /
  tribal advisory lineage reinforces credibility.)

> **Wedge thesis:** Federal money is flowing to Tribal *infrastructure*; the
> resulting Tribal *enterprises* (casinos, clinics, gov, schools) still need someone
> to broker the ongoing connectivity, transport, SD-WAN, UCaaS, and security on top
> of that infrastructure. That recurring layer is ours.

### 2.4 The data-center connectivity wedge

- The **AI-driven data-center buildout** is creating enormous demand for
  high-capacity **transport, dark fiber, wavelengths**, and **interconnection**
  between data centers, carrier hotels, and cloud on-ramps (**DCI**). Every new
  campus needs diverse, redundant, high-bandwidth network — and someone to source it.
- **How a broker monetizes:** be the **connectivity layer** for data-center
  developers and operators. Developers (e.g., the kind of clients who build campuses
  and assemble powered land) **build and strategize but do not source the network.**
  Konative steps in to broker DIA, dark fiber, waves, cross-connects, and cloud
  on-ramps into the facility — recurring revenue per site.
- **This is where the existing Konative dataset becomes a weapon:** we already track
  data-center projects, status, capacity, and geography across the U.S. and Canada
  (incl. First Nations projects). That's a **targeted prospect list of connectivity
  demand** no competing agent has.

### 2.5 Positioning & messaging that builds trust (what the best brokerages do)

Strong technology/telecom brokerage sites consistently lead with:
- **Vendor-neutral / "we work for you, not the carrier."** The #1 trust signal.
- **"Free to you — suppliers pay us."** Removes the cost objection up front.
- **Breadth of supplier portfolio** (logos / "100+ providers / one contract").
- **A clear process / "how it works"** (assess → source → design → procure →
  manage → support).
- **Verticals & proof** (named industries, outcomes, "single point of contact for
  the life of the account").
