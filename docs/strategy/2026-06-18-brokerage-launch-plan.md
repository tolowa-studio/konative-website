# Konative brokerage launch plan

**Decision date:** 2026-06-18  
**Operating company:** Total Pacific LLC, doing business as Konative  
**Channel model:** Vendor-neutral technology advisor and Avant sub-agent  
**Primary objective:** Build a durable book of monthly recurring connectivity revenue.

## Canonical positioning

Konative is a vendor-neutral connectivity brokerage for organizations that need
internet, fiber, transport, cloud, voice, security, mobility, colocation, or
data-center interconnection. Konative specializes in two markets where its
relationships and proprietary research create an advantage:

1. Tribal and rural enterprise connectivity.
2. Data-center connectivity across the United States and Canada.

Avant's supplier portfolio and Pathfinder support sourcing and transaction
execution. Konative owns the customer relationship, market intelligence,
solution design, qualification, and lifecycle service.

The map, award database, data-center database, and research library are lead
engines and trust assets. They are not presented as a real-time carrier-pricing
platform unless a source-backed product is actually operating.

## Revenue portfolio

### 1. Core residual brokerage

- Dedicated internet, business broadband, lit fiber, dark fiber and wavelengths.
- SD-WAN/SASE, managed network, firewall and cybersecurity.
- UCaaS, CCaaS, POTS replacement and mobility.
- Cloud on-ramps, colocation, cross-connects and data-center interconnection.
- Lifecycle renewals, upgrades, moves, adds, changes and escalation management.

**Commercial goal:** maximize contracted monthly recurring revenue and retain
the account through renewal.

### 2. Connectivity management retainer

A supplier-neutral managed-advisory layer for customers with multiple sites or
multiple vendors:

- inventory and contract calendar;
- invoice and renewal review;
- outage/escalation coordination;
- quarterly network and cost review;
- sourcing of replacements and additions.

Use a fixed monthly fee when the work extends beyond supplier-compensated
brokerage. Price after the first three design-partner engagements establish the
actual service load.

### 3. Tribal network portfolio review

A fixed-fee entry offer for Tribal governments, casinos, health systems,
schools, utilities and EDCs:

- current carrier and contract inventory;
- redundancy and single-point-of-failure review;
- grant-funded network-to-enterprise opportunity map;
- initial supplier and architecture options;
- 90-day procurement roadmap.

Credit some or all of the fee against a resulting brokerage engagement when
commercially appropriate.

### 4. Data-center connectivity readiness

For developers, operators, tenants and powered-land teams:

- carrier and fiber-path discovery;
- diverse-entry and transport architecture;
- cloud/IX/colo adjacency;
- preliminary budget and procurement sequence;
- carrier outreach and quote coordination through Avant.

This is distinct from the broader powered-land readiness audit. It can become a
smaller, faster front-door product that converts directly into recurring MRC.

### 5. Portfolio intelligence subscriptions

Do not start with a generic paywall. Begin with narrow paid products:

- quarterly Tribal Connectivity Award & Procurement Monitor;
- Data Center Connectivity Demand Watchlist;
- Travel/Hospitality Connectivity Opportunity Monitor, once the travel database
  is located, normalized, and connected to identifiable buying events;
- custom territory briefs for carriers, contractors, investors and suppliers.

Sell the first versions as analyst-supported subscriptions or retainers before
building software.

### 6. Referral and contractor channel

Use 1099 business-development contractors for qualification and appointment
setting, not unsupervised technical selling.

- Provide a narrow script and approved claims.
- Pay on qualified meetings and/or closed residual revenue, subject to the Avant
  agreement and applicable law.
- Keep customer ownership, quoting, proposal creation and supplier selection
  with Konative.
- Record every touch, disposition and next action in Twenty.

### 7. Media and sponsorship

Konative Dispatch can monetize after the audience is demonstrably qualified:

- supplier-sponsored research;
- issue sponsorship;
- webinars and briefing calls;
- underwriting of public data products.

Editorial findings and sponsor claims must remain clearly separated.

decisions harden?  
**CTA:** preliminary carrier/fiber-path review.

## Twenty CRM operating model

Each person should be linked to:

- organization;
- segment and campaign;
- award or market signal where applicable;
- owner;
- consent/source;
- last touch and next action;
- opportunity stage;
- expected MRC and one-time revenue;
- Avant/Pathfinder quote identifier;
- renewal date after close.

Recommended stages:

`Target → Researched → Approved for outreach → Contacted → Qualified →
Requirements received → Quoting → Proposal delivered → Verbal commit →
Ordered → Installed → Residual active → Renewal`

No automated outbound occurs before a record reaches **Approved for outreach**.

## Approval gates

Jeramey approval is required before:

- sending any outbound email or launching a sequence;
- enabling contractor access to a campaign;
- publishing externally quoted grant or market figures;
- representing access to an Avant API/SDK that has not been granted;
- proposing commission splits or contractor compensation;
- deploying pricing for new advisory retainers.
