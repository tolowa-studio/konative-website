# Konative contractor call playbook

## Purpose

The caller's job is to identify a real connectivity need and earn permission
for a Konative advisor to follow up. The caller does not design solutions,
promise savings, quote suppliers, discuss grant eligibility, or claim that
Konative represents a carrier.

## Opening

> Hi [Name], this is [Caller] working with Konative. We help Tribal and rural
> organizations compare and manage internet, fiber, voice, cloud, security and
> network providers. I’m calling because [approved organization-specific
> reason]. Did I catch you with thirty seconds?

## Discovery questions

1. Who owns carrier and connectivity decisions today?
2. Are there any renewals, new sites, broadband builds or network upgrades in
   the next twelve months?
3. Is redundancy or provider diversity a current concern?
4. Are voice, cybersecurity, cloud access or managed networking being reviewed
   alongside connectivity?
5. Would a carrier-neutral review be useful if it does not require changing
   providers?

## Award-matched context

> We saw the public TBCP award associated with [Nation/organization]. We are not
> calling about grant administration. We are trying to understand what
> enterprise and operating-network needs may follow the infrastructure build.

Do not state that award funds can be used for a product or service unless that
use has been independently verified.

## Qualified meeting

A meeting is qualified when there is:

- a named organization and decision owner;
- a current contract, project, pain point or planning window;
- a plausible connectivity or adjacent Avant-portfolio need;
- permission for a Konative advisor to follow up.

## Dispositions

- Qualified — meeting requested
- Qualified — requirements requested by email
- Future renewal — date known
- Referred to another contact
- No current need
- Not a fit
- Do not contact
- Bad data

Every call must be recorded in Twenty before the next record is dialed.
