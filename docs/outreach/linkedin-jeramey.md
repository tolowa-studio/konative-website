## 2 · Contrarian take on stalled projects

Hot take: **a "stalled" data center project is rarely a dead project.**

$64 billion of US data center projects sit blocked or delayed across 40
states. Other tallies have the construction-halted pool at $156B across 48
named projects. Cancellations quadrupled to 25 in 2025; Q1 2026 logged 20+
before the quarter closed.

But here's what's missing from the press: when the original sponsor walks,
the **land usually keeps its interconnection commitment**. The deal team
has sunk the diligence. The capital still wants a path.

That makes the asset re-tradable — quietly, fast — to a sponsor with a
better community story, a different cooling design, or a tribal-land
alternative.

If you're sitting on capital that wants infrastructure exposure, the stalled
pool is where the brokerage actually works.

## 3 · Behind-the-scenes — what a readiness audit actually does

A buyer once asked me: "what's actually in a $35K readiness audit?"

Honest answer — seven artifacts in 4–6 weeks:

1. Project framing brief (target capacity, capital, timeline — written down)
2. Site & infrastructure diligence (power, cooling, water, fiber, zoning)
3. Supply-chain & critical-path review (long-leads, sequencing pressure)
4. Risk register (delivery, regulatory, infrastructure, timing — IC-ready)
5. Decision-grade pro forma (the kind that holds up in an LP conversation)
6. Go / no-go recommendation (with named assumptions that would change it)
7. Executive readout (30-min live walkthrough)

What it isn't: a marketing deck or an MAI appraisal. It's the artifact you
take into the *next* decision — funding, partner, or commitment.

We built it productized and fixed-fee on purpose. Open-ended consulting
doesn't help when capital has a calendar.

## 4 · ERCOT growth chart (specific data anecdote)

CenterPoint Energy in Texas — one utility, in one region — saw large-load
interconnection requests grow **700% in a single year**.

1 GW → 8 GW.

Multiply that by every regional ISO. PJM, MISO, CAISO. That's the queue you
hear about. ~2,600 GW pending across the US.

What it means in practice: a developer with a fresh interconnection request
in 2026 is, statistically, getting power in 2034.

So the parcels worth real money are the ones that filed before the rush —
or are sitting on existing capacity headroom that nobody else has scored yet.
