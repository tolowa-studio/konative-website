## House style — quick reference

These rules are STRATEGY.md tone guidance applied to outreach:

- **No buzzwords.** "Disruptive," "seamless," "next-generation," "best-in-class" — out. "Powered land," "stalled," "go/no-go," "queue position," "comp" — in.
- **No marketing-speak in the subject.** "Quick question," "thoughts?", "[Name], 2 min?" — bin. "Loudoun comp + your parcel," "Re: [PROJECT NAME] — re-trade thesis," "ERCOT queue update + the audit" — in.
- **Lead with a fact, not a pitch.** First sentence is a number, a named deal, or a specific signal. Second sentence ties it to *them*.
- **One ask per email.** Either a 15-min call OR the /readiness-audit link. Never both.
- **Sign as a human.** "Scott Swartzbaugh / Konative" — not "The Konative Team."
- **150 words max** on a cold email. Less is better.

## The one CTA — always

`konative.com/readiness-audit` — $25K–$50K, 4–6 weeks, named team, sellable today.

If the email is doing its job, the destination is that URL. If the prospect
wants a call before the audit, they can request it through the form.

## Sequencing

Each prospect gets up to **three touches**, spaced 5–7 business days:

1. **Touch 1** — first-touch email (this kit). Pure signal + ask.
2. **Touch 2** — reply with a specific data point from this week (Konative Dispatch issue link works well here). Soft re-ask.
3. **Touch 3** — short "closing the loop" note. No ask. Wishes them well. Leaves the door open. This message gets the most replies of the three.

Track in CRM (or interim spreadsheet) — date, prospect, channel, response, next action.

## Tracking — what counts as a win

- **Loss:** no reply after 3 touches.
- **Soft win:** reply with timing pushback ("not now / Q1") → into the nurture list.
- **Real win:** call booked.
- **The win:** signed engagement letter for an R4 audit.

Target for days 0–30: **1 signed R4** (~$35K) and **3+ qualified call bookings**.
