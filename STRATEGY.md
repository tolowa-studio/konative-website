## The goal

Make **Konative a focused Avant sub-agent and vendor-neutral connectivity
brokerage with a durable book of monthly recurring revenue.** Konative sources
and manages internet, fiber, transport, cloud, voice, security, mobility,
colocation, and data-center interconnection for buyers across North America.

The two primary wedges are **Tribal and rural enterprise connectivity** and
**data-center connectivity**. The existing map, powered-land research,
interconnection data, funding intelligence, award records, and data-center
database remain strategic assets. They now function primarily as the lead and
trust engine behind the connectivity brokerage.

Monetization model (updated 2026-06-18): **residual brokerage first, supported by
paid advisory, lifecycle management, intelligence subscriptions, and media.**
The website is the qualification and conversion engine; Twenty is the CRM source
of truth; Avant/Pathfinder is the supplier-sourcing and transaction layer.

The previous three-sided powered-land brokerage strategy remains a secondary
data-center lane, not the company-wide primary message. See
`docs/strategy/2026-06-18-brokerage-launch-plan.md`.

## Why now (the market we are entering)

- Hyperscaler capex hit **~$413B in 2025**, tracking **$600–700B in 2026**. The
  binding constraint is not capital — it is **power**. The US interconnection
  queue backlog is **~2,600 GW**; projects coming online in 2025 averaged **~8
  years** in the queue.
- **"Powered land"** — parcels with secured interconnection — is now a named
  asset class. Land that traded at $10K–$30K/acre pre-boom now trades
  **$200K–$1M+/acre**. The term is young, rising, and **not yet owned by any
  Big-3 brokerage in search**. This is the category Konative claims.
- **Stalled / distressed projects** are a fast-growing pool: cancellations
  quadrupled to ~25 in 2025; **$64B–$156B of projects are blocked or delayed**
  on power, water, financing, permitting, or community opposition. Konative's
  `/governors` page already targets this — it is a core wedge, not a side page.
- The incumbents (CBRE, JLL, Cushman, Newmark, Colliers) are slow,
  relationship-gated, and siloed by service line. The pure-data players
  (datacenterHawk, DC Byte) sell intelligence but do not broker. **White space:
  a digital-native, content-led brokerage that owns "powered land" + "stalled
  projects" + transparent three-sided matching.**

## North stars (ranked)

1. **Organic discoverability.** Indexed, ranking, and AI-cited for "powered land"
   and the transactional long-tail. Ranked first **because the site is currently
   undiscoverable** — no sitemap, no robots.txt, no structured data. Nothing else
   in this plan works until crawlers and AI answer engines can see the ~30 pages
   already built. This is the compounding, zero-cost distribution moat.
2. **Qualified audience.** Newsletter subscribers who are *real* energy-infra
   principals — developers, investors, landowners, occupiers, advisors. Quality
   over quantity, exactly like SpokaneWire's "legitimate audience." A focused
   list of 2,000–5,000 qualified subs makes media monetization viable;
   5,000–15,000 makes it strong.
3. **Proprietary deal flow.** Qualified powered-land listings, stalled-project
   leads, occupier RFPs, and investor mandates captured through the site. This is
   the asset that produces commissions **and** makes the intelligence
   non-copyable. A brokerage with no inventory and no mandates is nothing.
4. **Revenue follows.** Commissions + newsletter sponsorships + intelligence
   subscriptions + readiness audits + retainers. Do not optimize revenue directly
   until #2 and #3 are real. Audience and deal flow are the leading indicators;
   revenue is the lagging one.

**Conversion order we believe in:**
`search / AI answer / LinkedIn / referral → konative.com visit → newsletter
signup (audience) OR deal-side form — list land / submit RFP / investor inquiry
(deal flow) → qualified conversation → transaction OR sponsor / subscriber.`

**The flywheel:** audience → proprietary deal flow → sharper intelligence →
bigger audience. Every bet should advance one edge of that loop.

## Revenue surfaces

Like SpokaneWire's five surfaces — multiple shots on goal, sequenced behind
audience proof.

| # | Surface | Economics | When |
|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | **Brokerage transaction commissions** — land sales, ground leases, build-to-suit, JV / capital advisory | ~2–4% on $50M–$200M deals ≈ **$1M–$8M per close**. Large, lumpy, slow. | Now, if a deal is already warm; otherwise the lagging prize |
| R2 | **Newsletter sponsorships** — power developers, EPC, BESS vendors, law firms, lenders | **$1.5K–$5K+ per slot** at niche scale; energy/infra B2B CPMs $50–$150 | Gated behind audience (~2K+ qualified subs) |
| R3 | **Paid market-intelligence subscription** — powered-land inventory DB, interconnection/queue tracker, quarterly reports | datacenterHawk / DC Byte are $10M–$25M-revenue proxies | Gated behind proprietary data (B5) |
| R4 | **Readiness audits & stalled-project diagnostics** — fixed-fee feasibility, interconnection viability, opposition-risk scoring | Fixed-fee advisory; converts traffic to cash **before** any close | Near-term cash; can sell now |
| R5 | **Investor / occupier retainers** — recurring monthly sourcing / market coverage | Predictable monthly revenue; de-risks the lumpy commission cycle | Once deal flow + intelligence are credible |
| R6 | **Featured listing / placement fees** — landowners & developers pay to feature powered or distressed sites | Marketplace listing model | Later |

Near-term cash: **R1** (if warm) and **R4** (sell audits off site traffic now).
**R2/R3/R5** are real money but stay gated behind audience and data proof — do
not sell them before the proof exists.

## Hypotheses (testable beliefs)

Each open bet has a Stash hypothesis under namespace
`/projects/konative/strategy`. The weekly review confirms or rejects each based
on telemetry (B11). Format — **Belief / Measurement / Threshold / Window**:

- **B1 (technical SEO):** indexing the foundation makes pages crawlable.
  *Measure:* pages indexed in Google Search Console. *Confirm:* 20+ pages
  indexed within 30 days of submit. *Reject:* <10 after 60 days.
- **B2 (powered land):** a GEO-optimized pillar page can rank/AI-cite for a young
  category. *Measure:* rank position + AI Overview citation for "powered land".
  *Confirm:* page 1 or AI-cited within 90 days. *Reject:* not top 30 after 120 days.
- **B3 (land-side long-tail):** landowner-intent long-tail is winnable for a new
  domain. *Measure:* organic visitors to `/land/*` + landowner-intent pages.
  *Confirm:* 50+ organic visitors/week within 90 days. *Reject:* <15 after 120.
- **B5 (proprietary data):** original data earns links + citations. *Measure:*
  referring domains + AI citations to the index/tracker. *Confirm:* 5+ referring
  domains within 90 days of first publish. *Reject:* <2 after 120.
- **B6 (newsletter):** a sharpened, well-placed CTA grows a qualified list.
  *Measure:* newsletter signups that match the principal profile. *Confirm:* 200+
  qualified subs within 90 days. *Reject:* <60 after 120.
- **B8 (deal-side conversion):** the four forms produce real, qualified inputs.
  *Measure:* qualified deal-side submissions (per the B8 rubric). *Confirm:* 1+
  qualified input/week within 60 days. *Reject:* 0 after 90.

Open bets without a hypothesis above (B4, B7, B9, B10, B11) get one written into
Stash when they move from planned to in-progress.

## Operating cadence

- **Weekly review:** read this file, then the telemetry snapshot. For each bet:
  ship, measure, or confirm/reject its hypothesis. Append the ledger. 3–5
  priorities for the week.
- **Monthly:** north-star review; retire or promote Later bets; reconcile the
  STRATEGY ledger into the MOTION GTM OS hub in Notion.
- **Meetings → execution:** decisions captured same day into the ledger + Stash.

### Agent-assisted execution
Build work runs on the SpokaneWire multi-agent wave model (worktrees +
file-ownership map + merge protocol) when a wave is large enough to warrant it.
Permission tiers for any agent touching this repo:

- **Allowed (commit + push):** copy, SEO meta, JSON-LD, new SEO/content pages,
  hypothesis confirm/reject in Stash, STRATEGY ledger appends.
- **Requires human approval (PR + ping):** new API routes that mutate state, DB
  migrations, CRM/n8n wiring, ad/budget changes, deletions >50 lines.
- **Hard-blocked:** secrets / `.env*`, force-push or history rewrite, sending to
  the newsletter list outside the Beehiiv pipeline.

A cron-driven autonomous strategy agent (as SpokaneWire runs) is **deferred** —
not set up in this phase. Revisit once the weekly loop is proven by hand.
